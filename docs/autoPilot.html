Motivation

Following my racing drone, I began developing my flying experience with fixed-wing aircraft. Using resources available from the community (designs, tips, etc.), I built and learned to fly the aircraft pictured on this page.

After taking more advanced controls courses, I was inspired to design an autopilot system and implement it in hardware. I envisioned a system where the user defined a sequence of waypoints for the autopilot to navigate.

After a proposal to my professor, I took this on as my capstone Senior Design Project, the culmination of the Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering Major at Cornell.

Summary

Being a one semester project, I had to restrain the scope of what I wanted to accomplish. Although I had already built a prototype by the beginning of the semester, I decided to put hardware development on hold and focus on analyzing and simulating the dynamics of the aircraft. The full 32 page report is linked below.

In summary, over the course of the semester I ...

  • Made a CAD model in Fusion 360 of the prototype
  • Analyzed the model in XFLR5, an airfoil analysis tool
  • Designed a 3 degree of freedom simulation in Simulink and used the results from XFLR5 to simulate the full system
  • Designed a full 6 degree of freedom simulation, also in Simulink
  • Incorporated a set of PID controllers and an LQR controller to track a desired climb rate or glide slope
  • Introduced a wind gust disturbance model to test the robustness of the controllers

Implementing Manual Control

To ensure safety throughout development, the autopilot system needs to have a manual override. Implementing manual control also gave me the opportunity to get a feel for how the prototype handles and how it can be improved.

Using SBUS protocol with Arduino

FrSky's SBUS protocol uses only one wire to send up to 18 channels from the radio transmitter to the receiver. To parse these data packets, there are decoders on the market that unpack each data packet into 4 separate PWM signals for servos. However, I will need more than 4 channels to control the aircraft, switch in and out of autopilot mode, arm and disarm the motor, etc. Thus, I decided to use an Arduino Nano to parse the SBUS packets and write PWM signals to the servos. A microcontroller will also be necessary for any computation involving autonomous control.
